Who actually receives food stamps?

Stories of CalFresh applicants — in their own voices

At Code for America, we run GetCalFresh.org, which helps more than 20,000 households apply for food assistance every month. We know that it's very difficult to overcome stigma and seek out help, even for people who are experiencing hunger. Despite their hardships, many of our users still feel compelled to describe the reluctance, and often shame, they feel about asking for help. They might mention they waited too long to apply, they have never asked for help before, or they hope to be off of food assistance as quickly as possible.

The stigma our users experience arises from myths about food assistance, which have a deep resonance in American culture. It only takes an occasional sensational anecdote for these myths to persist. Through this site, we will counteract those myths with real stories from GetCalFresh applicants, in their own words, that ground us in the facts about food assistance. We'll showcase real, representative stories of people seeking and receiving assistance, as well as relevant findings from rigorous research. We know that, as one of our users put it, "the less fortunate are invisible to so many people." Our aim is to surface their stories, with dignity and respect, and to make them visible.

Most GetCalFresh users were barely making ends meet when they were tipped into crisis by an unexpected expense or job loss.[1] The first set of stories on this site exemplify the precarious financial lives of many Californians. Once cost of living is taken into account, California is the state with the highest poverty rate.[2]

The second set of stories describes common shocks or adverse events that lead people to apply for food assistance. An illness, injury, loss of a job, a rent increase, or even a car breakdown might be enough to force a financially vulnerable household to begin making choices between food and other necessities.
